## Why this is suspicious

A 1.01 R:R ratio is almost mathematically impossible for a strategy with
asymmetric TPs (TP1 at 1.0R, TP2 at 2.0R). For wins to average exactly 1.01x
losses, either:
- ALL wins are at TP1 (1.0R) and ALL losses are at exactly the stop (1.0R)
- The wins are getting cut short before reaching TP1
- There's a calculation bug producing wrong R-multiples
- The strategy is exiting at random with no relationship to TP/stop logic

584 trades at 42% WR means ~245 wins and ~339 losses. With 1.01 R:R:
- avg_win × 245 - avg_loss × 339 = -$167
- If avg_loss = $X, avg_win = $1.01X
- 245 × 1.01X - 339X = -$167
- (247.45 - 339)X = -$167
- -91.55X = -$167
- X = $1.82 (avg loss size)

So average loss is $1.82, average win is $1.84. The strategy is essentially
breakeven per trade, just bleeding from frequency and fees.
