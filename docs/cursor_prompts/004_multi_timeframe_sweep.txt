You are setting up a comprehensive baseline sweep across multiple strategies
and timeframes to identify which combinations have real long-only edge.

## Context

We just fixed a long_only violation in the backtester. The clean baseline
for vwap_meanrev at 4h is now:
- 17 trades over 5 years (~3.4/year)
- 35.3% WR, 3.28 R:R, +$1.60 P&L
- Statistically unconfirmable at this trade frequency

We need to determine whether the strategy has more edge at different
timeframes, and whether OTHER strategies have stronger edges. This is
a sweep across (strategy × timeframe) to identify viable combinations
before doing any further optimization.

## Strategies and Timeframes to Test

NOTE: bull_flag strategies use intra-day timeframes; skip them for this sweep.

## Why this approach

If we just ran the sweep blindly:
- We might discover hours in that a strategy doesn't support 5m bars (crash)
- We might discover meanrev/htf_trend also have long_only bugs (junk data)
- We might run 9 long backtests when 3 would have told us the answer

Pre-flight catches structural issues; smoke tests catch runtime issues.
Then the actual sweep only runs the combinations we know will produce
meaningful data.
